JetHQ · Brand System v1

Brand Guidelines

A single warm gold identity. A charcoal ground. Two fonts. One topographic motif. A teal accent reserved for state. The brand is the HQ, not the jet.

Navigator / Steward Two-color logo Mrs Eaves + Gotham Quiet luxury

Logo Usage

The Mark

Insignia, not a tech logo

A winged-globe monogram paired with the JETHQ wordmark — JET set in a light serif, HQ set in a gold serif. The lockup is always a two-color treatment: ivory + gold on dark grounds; charcoal + gold on light grounds. Never paint it entirely gold. Never paint it entirely white. Never apply gradients or set it inside a containing shape.

On dark · Charcoal #414649

On light · Ivory #f5f1eb

Two-color rule

Always JET (ivory or charcoal) + HQ (gold). Never solid gold, never solid white, never gradients.

Clearspace

Reserve the cap height of the J in JET around the entire lockup. Nothing crosses that boundary.

Minimum size

Full lockup ≥ 140px wide. Globe alone ≥ 32px square. Below that, globe glyph only.

Never alter

No stretching, rotating, recoloring, outlining, embossing, drop-shadows, or containing shapes.

Typeface & Typography

Two Fonts

Display serif + clean sans

Mrs Eaves does the heavy lifting for headings and the wordmark. Gotham handles navigation, body, and all dense data — Light for body, Medium for UI. If brand-licensed fonts are unavailable, EB Garamond and Inter are mandatory free fallbacks. Three fonts in a JetHQ surface is a redesign, not a variation.

Color Usage

Five Swatches

Strict roles · no improvisation

Gold is identity, never interaction. Teal is state, never decoration. Charcoal is the page; cards descend toward black, not ascend toward white. Mist is the body text on dark. Pure white only lives inside the lockup containment box.

Gold is identity

The dominant brand color. Never paint a button gold by default — filled-gold is reserved for one hero CTA per page.

Teal is state

Active, selected, focus, info. The moment teal becomes decorative, its meaning as a state indicator is broken.

Cards go darker

On the charcoal ground, cards descend toward Deep Ink — not ascend toward white. The page reads like ink-on-paper.

Visual Accents

Topography

The only sanctioned ornament

Thin gold contour lines, like elevation rings on a survey map or isobars on a wind chart. They evoke terrain, navigation, and the quiet precision of route planning — the work behind a trip, not the trip itself. Maximum two topographic elements per visible viewport. Always low opacity. Never animated, never parallaxed, never over a photo.

Corner arrow ↙

A small bracket pointing southwest, placed at the top-right of every section heading. The JetHQ tick — quiet, technical, recurring.

1.5px stroke · 12–16px

Gold rules · top & bottom

1px gold hairline beneath the nav. 12px solid gold bar flush with the bottom edge. Structural frame on every full-page surface.

Restraint

Never more than two topographic elements per viewport. Never over a photograph. Never on light surfaces unless rendered in charcoal at low opacity.

Components

In Use

Buttons · status · data

Buttons are rectangles, 4px radius. Pills are reserved for status indicators and avatars. The primary button is a gold-outlined gold-text treatment on dark. Filled-gold exists but is rare — one hero CTA per page maximum.

House Rules

Do · Don't

Restraint over volume

Do

  • Default to the charcoal ground. The brand was designed for it.
  • Use exactly two fonts — display serif and sans.
  • Keep the gold hairline on top and the gold bar on the bottom.
  • Pair every section heading with a corner-arrow marker.
  • Reserve Signal Teal for state — active, selected, focus, info.
  • Use tabular figures in any dense data.
  • Show the broker — named, with credentials, real face, available light.

Don't

  • Don't paint the wordmark all-gold. Two-color only.
  • Don't paint a button gold by default. Outline-gold is the default; filled is for the hero CTA.
  • Don't use teal as a decorative accent. State only.
  • Don't make cards lighter than the page. Cards go darker, not lighter.
  • Don't use stock aviation imagery — tarmac handshakes, sunset tails, champagne cabins.
  • Don't use pills for buttons. Pills are for status and avatars.
  • Don't introduce a third font, glassmorphism, animated counters, or urgency banners.
